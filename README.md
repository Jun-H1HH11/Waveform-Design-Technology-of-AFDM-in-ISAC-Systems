# Waveform Design Technology of AFDM in ISAC Systems
中山大学硕士学位论文《通感一体化系统中的仿射频分复用波形设计技术》

Master thesis of Sun Yat-sen University: "Waveform Design Technology of AFDM in ISAC Systems", the chinese version provided.

通感一体化（Integrated Sensing and Communication, ISAC）作为未来无线通信系统的关键发展方向，对高效的波形设计技术提出了迫切需求。仿射频分复用（Affine Fourier Division Multiplexing, AFDM）因其独特的时频特性，被认为在高动态场景下具有巨大潜力。然而，目前对 AFDM 的内在感知机理研究尚不充分，缺乏针对性的信号处理框架来充分发挥其优势并处理其固有的时延多普勒（Delay Doppler, DD）耦合效应，同时，其关键参数对通感性能的联合影响以及在实际物理信道中的性能表现也有待深入研究与验证。 为此，本文深入研究了 AFDM 波形在 ISAC 系统中的设计问题，旨在填补上述空白，充分挖掘并利用 AFDM 的内在优势，在确保通信可靠性的前提下，显著提升其雷达感知性能。

首先，为解决 AFDM 感知机理不明的问题，本文从 AFDM 子载波的物理特性入手，揭示并严格证明了其与雷达 Chirp 信号之间深刻的内在等价关系，明确了等效参数条件。这一发现为 AFDM 感知提供了坚实的理论基础，并从根本上阐明了其抗多径与多普勒效应的潜力。 针对缺乏专用处理框架和 DD 耦合的挑战， 本文提出了在 DD 域对 AFDM 信号进行处理的框架，并建立了相应的 DD 域信号模型与输入输出关系。该框架不仅显著简化了模糊函数的推导，更重要的是能够显式处理并有效克服 DD 耦合现象，为高性能接收机设计铺平了道路。 在此基础上，本文针对性地设计了三种基于子载波的有效感知算法即类 DeChirp 算法、类 Chirp 匹配滤波算法和 DD 域匹配滤波算法，这些算法为不同数据干扰程度和复杂度要求的场景提供了灵活的技术选择，实现了性能与复杂度的有效权衡。

然后，考虑到实际部署中的兼容性需求，本文研究并提出了基于 AFDM 符号设计的 ISAC 方案。通过探讨感知前导序列设计和参数优化，成功使 AFDM 符号在保持其固有通信优势的同时，获得了接近正交频分复用（Orthogonal Frequency Division Multiplexing, OFDM）的优异测距性能，有效解决了 AFDM 感知性能与兼容性的平衡问题。 结合脉冲多普勒雷达思想设计的低复杂度感知算法以及感知辅助信道估计与均衡（Sensing aided Channel Estimation, SACE）方案，进一步验证了该符号级方案在提升通信性能方面的实用价值。 对无前导序列场景的分析也拓展了其应用范围。

进一步地，为验证理论与算法在真实环境下的有效性，本文不局限于纯理论分析和计算机仿真，基于通用软件无线电外设（Universal Software Radio Peripheral, USRP） 硬件平台和 LabVIEW 成功搭建了 AFDM-ISAC 原型样机。特别地，设计并实现了一款旋转天线测试台，能够物理模拟真实可控的高速移动，解决了快时变信道难以在实验室复现的难题。 利用该平台对所提子载波级和符号级方案进行了全面的原型验证，实验结果强有力地证实了所提理论、模型与算法在实际物理信道环境下的有效性与鲁棒性。

本文的研究工作为 AFDM 波形在 ISAC 系统中的应用提供了从基础理论、信号处理框架到算法设计与原型验证的完整解决方案， 揭示了 AFDM 作为一种面向高动态 ISAC 场景的潜力波形的巨大价值。研究成果不仅为后续 AFDM-ISAC 的深入研究和标准化提供了重要参考，也启发了将雷达处理思想与先进通信波形设计相结合的新思路。

Integrated Sensing and Communication (ISAC), as a pivotal development direction for future wireless communication systems, presents urgent demands for efficient waveform design techniques. Affine Fourier Division Multiplexing (AFDM), owing to its unique time-frequency characteristics, has demonstrated significant potential in high-mobility scenarios. However, current research inadequately addresses the inherent sensing mechanisms of AFDM, lacks dedicated signal processing frameworks to fully exploit its advantages while addressing the intrinsic Delay-Doppler (DD) coupling effects, and requires deeper investigation into the joint impacts of key parameters on communication-sensing performance as well as practical performance evaluation in physical channels. This paper systematically investigates AFDM waveform design for ISAC systems to address these gaps, aiming to fully exploit the inherent advantages of AFDM while ensuring communication reliability and significantly enhancing radar sensing performance.

To resolve the ambiguity in the sensing mechanism of AFDM, we commence with analyzing the physical characteristics of AFDM subcarriers, rigorously revealing and proving their fundamental equivalence with radar Chirp signals through explicit parameter mapping. This discovery establishes a theoretical foundation for AFDM-based sensing and fundamentally explains its inherent resistance to multipath and Doppler effects. Addressing the challenges of dedicated processing framework development and DD coupling mitigation, we propose a novel DD-domain signal processing framework with corresponding signal models and input-output relationships. This framework not only simplifies ambiguity function derivation but also explicitly resolves DD coupling issues, paving the way for high-performance receiver design. Building on this, we develop three efficient subcarrier-based sensing algorithms: DeChirp-like algorithm, Chirp-matched filtering algorithm, and DD-domain matched filtering algorithm, providing flexible technical solutions for scenarios with varying data interference levels and complexity requirements while maintaining effective performance-complexity tradeoffs.

Considering practical deployment compatibility requirements, we propose an ISAC scheme based on AFDM symbol design. Through optimized sensing preamble design and parameter optimization, AFDM symbols achieve ranging performance comparable to orthogonal frequency division multiplexing (OFDM) while preserving inherent communication advantages, effectively balancing sensing performance and system compatibility. The proposed low-complexity sensing algorithm inspired by pulsed Doppler radar principles and the sensing-aided channel estimation and equalization (SACE) scheme further demonstrate the practical value of this symbol-level design in enhancing communication performance. Additional analysis of preamble-free scenarios extends its application scope.

To validate theoretical findings under realistic conditions, we transcend conventional theoretical analysis and computer simulations by implementing an AFDM-ISAC hardware prototype using Universal Software Radio Peripheral (USRP) platforms and LabVIEW. Notably, we design a rotating antenna testbed that physically emulates controllable high-speed Doppler effects, overcoming laboratory implementation challenges for high-mobility channels. Comprehensive prototype verification of both subcarrier-level and symbol-level schemes through this platform provides strong experimental evidence for the effectiveness and robustness of proposed theories, models, and algorithms in practical physical channels.

This research provides a complete solution encompassing fundamental theory, signal processing frameworks, algorithm design, and prototype validation for AFDM-based ISAC systems, revealing the significant potential of AFDM as a promising waveform design for high-mobility ISAC scenarios. The outcomes not only serve as crucial references for subsequent AFDM-ISAC research and standardization but also inspire novel approaches integrating radar processing principles with advanced communication waveform design.
